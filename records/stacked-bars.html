Stacked bars, and what the tooltip carries

A tooltip is a disclosure tier, not a dumping ground: the values a decision turns on stay on the canvas, the tooltip only enriches them. And a segment too thin to see is still reachable — the hit target is the whole column, never the paint.

Our context

VC's stacked bars carry payer mix, gap status and risk tier — four to six categories across a handful of columns, read by analysts and care managers on large desktop monitors. The smallest segment is routinely the one that decides the work — the 2% cohort still open on a quality gap — and it is exactly the band the pointer cannot catch.

The rule (provisional)

Sort a value into the tier that has earned it. Every fact a decision needs is always visible; the tooltip is the second tier, not the first copy.

  • Always visible — the axis, the total, and a direct label for any value the decision turns on.
  • On demand (tooltip) — exact figures, denominators, definitions. Enrichment, never the only copy of a needed fact.
  • On navigate — the full row set, history, provenance. A table or a detail page, not a tooltip.

The hover and focus target is the column, not the segment. Give every column a hit band of at least 24px, whatever the paint does.

03 / Where the tooltip sits

Anchored to the column it describes

The tooltip anchors to the column, sits above it, and never covers the mark it describes. The reader points at a stack and the enrichment appears clear of the paint — so the shape being read stays visible while its exact figures are read off.

  • Above the column, offset by a gap. The tip clears the top of the stack; the offset gap is part of the hoverable box, so moving from column to card never dismisses it mid-travel.
  • Clamped inside the plot. The first and last columns push their tips flush to the plot edge instead of overflowing it; a full-height column flips its tip below rather than clip on the board top.
  • Reachable by keyboard. Every column is focusable and shows the same card on :focus-visible as on hover, with a visible focus ring.

Every value lives in the table above; the tooltip only saves a glance. That is what keeps this an honest demo rather than a broken one — the tip conveys nothing not already on the page, so WCAG 1.4.13 (content on hover) does not apply. A production tooltip carrying anything unique would additionally owe Escape-to-dismiss, which needs a key handler this no-JS site does not ship. On touch there is no :hover and :focus-visible will not fire on a tap — a stated assumption, not an oversight: VC's users are analysts and config admins on large desktop monitors, and the table carries the data on any device regardless.
